Ad-Hoc Problems

Ad-hoc problems are those that do not require specific algorithms, and don't fall into specified categories. These problems present novel challenges and require careful reading. When facing these problems, it is a good idea to spend time to find a smart solution before coding. While some ad-hoc problems might be simple, others can require deep analysis in order to be solved.


Example Problems

The best way to master ad-hoc problems is to spend a lot of time to practice solving these problems. Here are a couple good examples (sorted by difficulty)[1]:

  • COW
  • Breed Proximity
